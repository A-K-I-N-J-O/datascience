datascience.tables.Table.with_relabeling¶

Table.with_relabeling(column_label, new_label)[source]¶

Returns a new table with column_label changed to new_label.

column_label and new_label may be single values or lists specifying column labels to be changed and their new corresponding labels.

Args:
column_label (single str or list/array of str): The label(s) of
columns to be changed. Must be str.
new_label (single str or list/array of): The new label(s) of

columns to be changed. Must be str.

Number of elements must match number of elements in column_label.

Returns:
A new separate table containing same data with column headers in column_label replaced by new_label.
>>> letter = ['a', 'b', 'c', 'z']
>>> count = [9, 3, 3, 1]
>>> points = [1, 2, 2, 10]
>>> table = Table([letter, count, points], ['letter', 'count', 'points'])
>>> table
letter | count | points
a      | 9     | 1
b      | 3     | 2
c      | 3     | 2
z      | 1     | 10
>>> table2 = table.with_relabeling('points', 'minions')
>>> table2
letter | count | minions
a      | 9     | 1
b      | 3     | 2
c      | 3     | 2
z      | 1     | 10
>>> table
letter | count | points
a      | 9     | 1
b      | 3     | 2
c      | 3     | 2
z      | 1     | 10
